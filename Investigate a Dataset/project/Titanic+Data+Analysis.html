Background Information¶

The sinking of the RMS Titanic occurred on the night of 14 April through to the morning of 15 April 1912 in the North Atlantic Ocean, four days into the ship's maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City. The largest passenger liner in service at the time, Titanic had an estimated 2,224 people on board when she struck an iceberg at around 23:40 (ship's time) on Sunday, 14 April 1912. Her sinking two hours and forty minutes later at 02:20 (05:18 GMT) on Monday, 15 April resulted in the deaths of more than 1,500 people, which made it one of the deadliest peacetime maritime disasters in history. (Wikipedia)

One of the reasons that the shipwreck led to such loss of life was that there were not enough lifeboats for the passengers and crew. Although there was some element of luck involved in surviving the sinking, some groups of people were more likely to survive than others, such as women, children, and the upper-class. (Kaggle)

Questions for Investigation¶

  1. Is buying a 1st class ticket can increase the chances of survival?
  2. Does age affect the chances of survival? Because in general, a young age has a better physical strength to survive.
  3. Who has a higher chance of survival between male and female?
  4. Is having a sibling or a spouse can increase the chances of survival?
  5. What are the chances of survival if having children or parents?
  6. What are the chances of survival of passengers departing from Cherbourg?

Dataset¶

The Titanic Dataset contains 891 passengers that survive or not from the disaster. On the dataset there are some details passenger data as follows:

PassengerId     Passenger Id
Survived        Survival
                (0 = No; 1 = Yes)
Pclass          Passenger Class
                (1 = 1st; 2 = 2nd; 3 = 3rd)
Name            Name of passenger
Sex             Sex
                (male or female)
Age             Age
SibSp           Number of Siblings/Spouses Aboard
Parch           Number of Parents/Children Aboard
Ticket          Ticket Number
Fare            Passenger Fare
Cabin           Cabin
Embarked        Port of Embarkation
                (C = Cherbourg; Q = Queenstown; S = Southampton)

The dataset can be obtained on Kaggle.

Is buying a 1st class ticket can increase the chances of survival?

Answer:

Conclusion¶

Passengers who had purchased first-class tickets, at age under 20 or at 30-60, a women, with siblings or spouses, with 1-3 children or parents, and departing from the port Cherbourg would have a higher percentage of survival rate.

Factors of Survival¶

Passengger Class

Based on data investigation, the first class passengers have higher chances to survive on the Titanic. The first class passengers may close to emergency routes and have better facilities. Also, when the accident happened the iceberg crushed the bottom side of Titanic, it might be where the 3rd-class passengers had been there. So, it could make the 3rd-class passengers have the lowest survival rate.

Age

From the statistical data, it shown that children (age 0-10 years old) have the biggest chance to survive. Maybe the children given the first priority in terms of life boats. While the young adult/adult (age 20-40 years old) also have more survival rate, it happened maybe they have more physical strength.

Gender

It is clearly shown that females have a bigger chance of surviving on the Titanic than a males (Females 78.6% Males 21.4%). It happened maybe female passengers get first priority in terms of life boats and get help from male passengers.

Siblings and Spouses

From the statistical data, it shown that passengers who have siblings at least 1 or have spouses have the larger of survival rate on Titanic. It happened maybe the passengers got help from their siblings or spouses.

Parent and Children

Passengers with less than 4 family (parent and/or children) have a larger survival chances. Probably because 2 young adults (parent) help 1 or more kids, or it could be the young adulst help their parent.

Port of Embarked

Passengers who departed from port of Cherbourg have a bigger chance of surviving on Titanic based on our statistical data. But, we could not conclude from the that data because it has not enough information the affect of Embarked location.
